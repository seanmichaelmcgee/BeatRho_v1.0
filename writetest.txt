About Robert Frost

Robert Frost (1874-1963) was one of America's most celebrated poets whose work is known for its realistic depictions of rural life in New England, use of American colloquial speech, and examination of complex social and philosophical themes. His poetry often explored the relationship between humans and nature, the passing of traditional rural life, and made observations about the human condition through simple, yet profound imagery.

Frost received four Pulitzer Prizes for Poetry and was awarded the Congressional Gold Medal in 1960. His most famous works include "The Road Not Taken," "Stopping by Woods on a Snowy Evening," and "Mending Wall." Many of his poems have become cultural touchstones, frequently quoted and referenced in discussions about life choices, nature, and the American experience. His poetry combines traditional verse forms with colloquial language, creating a distinctive voice that speaks with clarity about the complexities of life.

En un lugar de la Mancha, de cuyo nombre no quiero acordarme, no ha mucho tiempo que vivía un hidalgo de los de lanza en astillero, adarga antigua, rocín flaco y galgo corredor. Una olla de algo más vaca que carnero, salpicón las más noches, duelos y quebrantos los sábados, lantejas los viernes, algún palomino de añadidura los domingos, consumían las tres partes de su hacienda.

Contemplaba con melancolía sus libros de caballerías, aquellos tomos que habían transportado su espíritu a mundos de fantasía y gloria. Las hazañas de caballeros andantes como Amadís de Gaula y Palmerín de Inglaterra habían encendido en su alma un fuego que no se apagaba con el paso de los años. Soñaba despierto con ser como ellos, con restaurar la antigua orden de caballería y recorrer los caminos en busca de aventuras, desfaciendo agravios y enderezando tuertos, tal como lo hicieron sus admirados héroes.
